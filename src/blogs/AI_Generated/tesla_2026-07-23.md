# TESLA: A New Approach to Time Series Analysis

Introduction
------------

Time series analysis is a fundamental task in data science, involving the examination and forecasting of sequential data. However, traditional methods for time series analysis are limited in their ability to handle complex and non-linear patterns in the data. In this blog post, we will introduce TESLA, a new approach to time series analysis that leverages the power of graph neural networks (GNNs) to model and forecast time series data with unprecedented accuracy.
Background
------------

Traditional methods for time series analysis include:

1. Autoregressive (AR) models: These models predict the next value in a time series based on the previous values.
2. Moving Average (MA) models: These models predict the next value in a time series based on the average of the previous values.
3. Autoregressive Integrated Moving Average (ARIMA) models: These models combine the features of AR and MA models and also account for the integrated nature of the time series.
4. Seasonal ARIMA (SARIMA) models: These models extend the ARIMA models to handle time series with seasonality.
While these methods have been successful in many applications, they have some limitations. For example, they can struggle to handle complex and non-linear patterns in the data, and they may not perform well when the time series has multiple seasonalities.
Motivation
------------

The motivation behind TESLA is to develop a new approach to time series analysis that can handle complex and non-linear patterns in the data, while also being able to capture multiple seasonalities. TESLA leverages the power of GNNs to model and forecast time series data in a more accurate and efficient way than traditional methods.
Architecture
------------

The architecture of TESLA consists of three main components:

1. Time Series Encoder: This component is responsible for encoding the time series data into a graph structure. The graph structure captures the sequential and temporal relationships between the data points.
2. Graph Neural Network (GNN): This component is responsible for modeling the graph structure and making predictions about the future values of the time series. The GNN is trained on the graph structure and the corresponding time series data.
3. Prediction Module: This component is responsible for making predictions about the future values of the time series based on the output of the GNN.
