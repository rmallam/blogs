1. **Data Acquisition and Preprocessing Module**: This module is responsible for collecting data from various sources, such as weather forecasts, energy consumption data, and occupancy patterns. The data is then preprocessed and fed into the TESLA engine.
2. **TESLA Engine**: This is the core component of TESLA, which uses a combination of machine learning and control theory to optimize energy consumption. The TESLA engine predicts energy consumption patterns and optimizes energy usage in real-time.
3. **Control and Optimization Module**: This module is responsible for implementing the optimization algorithms and control strategies to achieve the desired energy savings. The module also provides a user interface for monitoring and controlling the energy management system.
## Algorithms

TESLA uses a combination of machine learning algorithms and control theory to optimize energy consumption. These algorithms include:

1. **Supervised Learning Algorithms**: TESLA uses supervised learning algorithms, such as linear regression, decision trees, and neural networks, to predict energy consumption patterns. These algorithms are trained on historical data to identify patterns and trends in energy consumption.
2. **Control Theory Algorithms**: TESLA uses control theory algorithms, such as model predictive control (MPC) and linear quadratic regulator (LQR), to optimize energy consumption. These algorithms are used to control the energy consumption of buildings based on the predicted patterns.
3. **Deep Learning Algorithms**: TESLA also uses deep learning algorithms, such as recurrent neural networks (RNNs) and convolutional neural networks (CNNs), to predict energy consumption patterns. These algorithms are particularly useful for predicting energy consumption patterns in buildings with complex HVAC systems.
